# Data Sources

Open Plovdiv uses reviewed JSON files for project and budget records, not live scraping. Every record links to a public source. Citizen problem reports are stored separately and appear publicly only after moderation.

## Known limitations (June 2026)

- **2026 is provisional.** The 2026 capital programme was adopted as a forecast estimate (прогнозен разчет) and is given here in euros; figures may change once the state budget is adopted. Such records are marked `provisional`.
- **2025 sector amounts are approximate.** The per-sector lev figures are derived from the published percentage shares of the ~139.6M лв capital programme, not from a line-by-line table.
- **Older budgets mix document formats.** The 2011-2018 archive uses council decisions and scanned PDFs where needed. Exact source URLs live on each `data/curated/budget-items.json` record; some scanned values were visually checked from the original document images.
- **2002 is adopted budget plus partial-year audit context.** The 2002 rows use the adopted annual budget and capital-expenditure figures cited by the National Audit Office. The same document audits execution only for 01.01-30.09.2002 and includes accounting-control caveats, so its nine-month execution totals are not plotted as a full-year result.
- **2005-2007 capital values are executed figures.** The capital chart includes realised capital expenditure from the 2009 functional analysis. They are labelled as execution and should not be read as the initially adopted capital programme.
- **2007-2013 OPR capital investment is an aggregate.** The subsequent evaluation of the municipal development plan reports 260.895M лв executed capital-investment expenditure for the whole period. It is stored as an archive programme record, not as annual budget totals for 2008 or 2009.
- **EU operational-programme figures are project approvals.** The 2007-2013 EU project totals come from a 2014 municipal post and describe approved project values, grants, municipal co-financing and received-to-date funds. They are not annual municipal budgets.
- **2008 draft budget is secondary-source context.** The 218.3M лв total and 73,064,443 лв capital figure from `gradat.bg` are contemporary figures for the municipality's proposed budget and capital priorities. They are plotted as `provisional`/draft chart rows and stored as archive context, not as adopted or executed annual totals.
- **2009 adopted budget is media-sourced.** The 260,702,383 лв adopted total and 77M лв capital-expenditure figure come from a contemporary Plovdiv24.bg report on the 5 March 2009 vote. The official municipal decision and appendices were not found in the public budget archive during the 17 June 2026 search, so the rows are marked as public-source records with that limitation.
- **2010 targeted decrees are not full budgets.** State Gazette decrees are used for the 2.859767M лв waste-linked infrastructure transfer and the later 2M лв reallocation to the City House of Culture. These are stored as programme records, not total budget rows.
- **Waste-linked compensation records overlap.** The archive includes individual instalments, object lists and the aggregate 26.5M лв figure for the 2009-2012 Sofia-waste compensation chain. These cards document the chain and should not be added together as separate spending totals.
- **2010 is execution, not adoption.** The 2010 row is the reported execution of the consolidated budget at 31.12.2010, taken from Municipal Council Decision 58 and audit context from the National Audit Office.
- **Pre-1990 amounts are historical nominal values.** The older finance records in `data/curated/city-archive.json` come from Regional History Museum studies. They preserve the source figures but are not inflation-adjusted and should not be compared directly with modern BGN totals.
- **Programme amounts are not always full programme budgets.** Some archive cards show targeted allocations or individual grant ranges, such as 1947 fair funding, 1969 Old Plovdiv funding, and the 2010 gifted-children support rule. They are labelled as programme records rather than full municipal budgets.
- **1999 culture records are programme scale, not municipal spending.** The European Month of Culture records include programme scale, participant counts, event counts and venue-repair context, but no total municipal budget or expenditure statement.
- **Audit-derived governance records are contextual.** The 1998-2005 and 2006-2008 National Audit Office documents are used to document planning, property-management and financial-management changes. They are not used as replacements for missing annual budget decisions.
- **City archive is source-backed, not interpretive.** `data/curated/city-archive.json` stores official mayor terms and a small set of event/programme records. It does not infer party affiliation, policy responsibility, or biographical claims.
- **No per-project completion list.** The municipality does not publish a single, citable "completed in 2025" status per project. Statuses here reflect what is sourced and each project page shows a source-backed timeline where available: started/continuing, new for 2026, or postponed (frozen with BGN 0 for 2025–2026).
- **Locations are approximate**, placed by street/district and marked as such on each project.
- `data/curated/fix-reports.json` is intentionally empty; the live map uses approved citizen reports from the report store.
